Degree

Definition: Degree represents the number of direct connections of one node with another in graph.

What it tells us: Nodes to a greater degree tell us that there are a lot of direct connections to other nodes in the graph. On the other hand, a lesser degree tells us that there are few direct connections between these nodes.

Visualization Context: The degree of each node is used to determine its size when generating the visualization. To prevent disproportionate views, a relative size is applied to each node.

 Community

Definition: Communities are groups of nodes that are very likely to share common properties on a network. A related theme is community detection, where the goal is to find a community that a certain node belongs to.

What it tells us: The more general definition is based on the principle that pairs of nodes are more likely to be connected if they are both members of the same community (ies), and less likely to be connected if they do not share communities.

Visualization Context: The Louvain algorithm is used to determine the community a node belongs, resulting in different colors to ilustrate these groups. All definitions collected from Book Graph Theory with Applications - John Adrian Bondy, U. S. R. Murty
